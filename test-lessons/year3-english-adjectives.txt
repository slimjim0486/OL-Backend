Year 3 English: Using Adjectives to Describe

Learning Objective: Use adjectives to make sentences more interesting.

What is an Adjective?
An adjective is a word that describes a noun. It tells us more about a person, place, or thing.

Examples:
- The big dog barked loudly.
- She wore a beautiful red dress.
- The old castle stood on the hill.

In these sentences, "big", "beautiful", "red", and "old" are all adjectives.

Types of Adjectives:
1. Size: big, small, tiny, huge, enormous
2. Colour: red, blue, green, yellow, purple
3. Shape: round, square, flat, curved
4. Feeling: happy, sad, angry, excited, scared

Practice Activity:
Add an adjective to make these sentences more interesting:

1. The ___ cat sat on the mat.
2. I ate a ___ apple for lunch.
3. We visited a ___ museum yesterday.
4. The ___ bird flew over the garden.

Remember: Good writers use adjectives to paint pictures with words!

Vocabulary Words:
- Adjective: a describing word
- Noun: a naming word (person, place, or thing)
- Describe: to tell what something is like
