Year 2 Science: What Plants Need to Grow

Learning Objective: Find out and describe how plants need water, light, and a suitable temperature to grow and stay healthy.

What Do Plants Need?
Plants are living things. Like us, they need certain things to grow and stay healthy.

Plants need:
1. Water - to drink and stay alive
2. Light - from the sun to make food
3. Air - to breathe
4. Warmth - not too hot, not too cold
5. Space - room for roots and leaves to grow

How Plants Drink:
Plants drink water through their roots. The roots are underground and soak up water from the soil. The water travels up the stem to the leaves.

How Plants Make Food:
Plants make their own food using sunlight! This is called photosynthesis. The leaves catch the sunlight and use it to make food for the plant.

Experiment Idea:
Try growing two bean plants:
- Give one plant water and put it in sunlight
- Give the other plant water but keep it in the dark

What do you think will happen? Why?

Key Words:
- Roots: the part of a plant underground that drinks water
- Stem: the part that holds the plant up
- Leaves: the part that catches sunlight
- Sunlight: light from the sun
- Healthy: strong and growing well
